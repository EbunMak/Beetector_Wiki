Design

splA Plasmid


splA - S-layer protein A - Paenibacillus larvae
The splA S-layer protein is found to be key to the virulence of ERIC II genotypes of P. larvae, as we’ve heard from HP contacts the ERIC II genotype is not present in Alberta or North America for that matter. Thus we looked to find a protein in the ERIC I or more genotypes of P. larvae. Potential proteins that are important to the virulence strategies of P. larvae have been identified in this 2019 study of exoproteins, such as toxins Plx1 and Plx2, chitin-degrading protein PlCBP49, ADP-ribosyltransferase C3larvin toxin, and Plx2A has been shown to be a C3-like toxin. For the purposes of this wiki assignment, we have chosen to use splA due to more literature being present on it but acknowledge we would look into the proteins listed above or perhaps others to find a more appropriate protein. This protein will be secreted and purified through the use of the His-tag attached, we can then place the protein onto a matrix for aptamer-binding.

Cas12a Plasmid


CRISPR-associated endonuclease Cas12a - Francisella tularensis subsp. novicida (strain U112)
Part:BBa_K2872019
The reason we choose Cas12a instead of Cas9 is that Cas9 requires two RNA molecules: tracrRNA and a crRNA, whereas Cas12a requires only a single RNA molecule. Even though Cas9s tend to have naturally higher fidelity, Cas12 is more compact and efficient. It also processes its own guide RNAs, leading to increased multiplexing ability. This protein will be secreted and then purified through the use of the His-tag attached, there is also a protease recognition site that will allow us to snip off the His-tag to allow proper functionality of Cas12a.

About the Protein


splA has not been recombinantly expressed before but Cas12a has as it is part of the CRISPR system. Thus there is more literature on the functionality of Cas12a and more research will need to be done for splA or whichever protein gets selected. splA is a membrane bound protein as it is used by the P. larvae to infect host cells through adhesion and confirmation change. Cas12a is not membrane bound as it is part of the CRISPR system.



Structure


The importance of the interplay between hydrophobic and hydrophilic regions were studied by reassembling the S‐layer protein SbpA on self‐assembled monolayers (SAMs) on gold composed of disulfides with different end groups [hydroxyl (OH) vs. methyl (CH3) groups] and lengths of the individual methylene chains (Moreno‐Flores et al., 2008). The formation of monolayers was observed when the hydrophobic end groups (CH3) overcame the hydrophilic (OH) ones. On the contrary, double S‐layers were formed when hydrophilic (OH) groups superseded the hydrophobic (CH3) end groups. S-layers represent highly porous protein lattices (30–70% porosity) with pores of uniform size and morphology in the 2–8 nm range.
