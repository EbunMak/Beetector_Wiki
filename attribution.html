Team Attributions


Human Practice Contacts

Dr. Elemir Simko and his research team colleagues, Professor at the University of Saskatchewan with a research area in honey bee health and sustainability in agriculture, for shaping our project ideation journey by suggesting we shift our focus from diagnosis to surveillance and detection of AFB in honey for industries since there is a need for a sensitive method to test low quantities of spores in extraction facilities.

Dr. Patricia Wolf Veiga, Acting Manager for the Grande Prairie Regional College National Bee Diagnostic Center in Beaverlodge, Alberta with her main research area in the field of bee health and molecular diagnostics, for informing us of the secretive ‘culture’ among farmers. If they have an infected beehive they are unlikely to report it and tell others due to the competitive nature among other farmers in the field. Additionally, she offered our team free data, sequences and samples which greatly impacted our project viability.
