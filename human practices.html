HUMAN CENTERED DESIGN


How can we solve problems that are affecting beekeepers?

AFB is the most detrimental honey brood disease and is endemic to Canada due to its infectious nature, with an outbreak of AFB potentially costing up to $18 million dollars. Our goal with Beesensor is to work towards finding a solution to this devastating disease. However, we recognize that we can not achieve this goal in solitude. Therefore, we hope to work directly with farmers and collaborate on a solution.


Required Materials:

Required materials include:

- Three electrical connectors: working electorode (WE), counter electrode (CE), reference electrode (RE).
- Other components of screen-printed electrode which include a substrate, conducting paths, auxillary electrode, and an insulation layer.
- Gold nanoparticles and a device to apply the nanoparticles to the electrode.
- Diagnostic device (electrochemical impedance spectrometer) and electronic device for data display.


Required Skills:

Required skills include:

- Foundational knowledge of electronic circuits and how the components interact with one another to receive signals.
- Foundational knowledge of how the nanoparticles interact with the electrode.
- Ability to utilize the software to connect to the sensor in order to interpret impedance meausurements.
- Ability to understand the output data and communicate to users in a manner familiar to them.
